## Cluster Tier Profiles

Two business-facing tiers were built from the locked k=2 clustering output:

- **Stable Core (Cluster 0):** 152 listings (69.1% of inventory), ADR $148, occupancy 46.9%, RevPAR $71. This tier is smaller-format inventory with stronger occupancy, lower revenue volatility, and a higher superhost share.
- **Premium Upside (Cluster 1):** 68 listings (30.9% of inventory), ADR $289, occupancy 34.4%, RevPAR $99. This tier is larger-format inventory with higher revenue potential, but also higher monthly volatility.

## Demand-Period Takeaways

- Stable Core average monthly revenue rises from $1330 in off-peak periods to $1549 in shoulder periods.
- Premium Upside average monthly revenue rises from $1805 in off-peak periods to $2143 in shoulder periods.
- Both tiers monetize best in shoulder periods, which lines up with the team's temporal demand findings.

## Portfolio Recommendation

For a 10 to 20 unit host, the most balanced recommendation is to keep roughly 30% of units in Premium Upside and 70% in Stable Core. That mix sacrifices some top-end revenue versus an all-premium portfolio, but it preserves occupancy stability, lowers annualized revenue volatility, and keeps off-peak revenue more resilient.

Recommended mixes:

- **10 units:** 7 Stable Core + 3 Premium Upside | expected TTM revenue $319,963, annualized volatility $20,276, risk-adjusted score 15.78.
- **15 units:** 10 Stable Core + 5 Premium Upside | expected TTM revenue $486,025, annualized volatility $25,296, risk-adjusted score 19.21.
- **20 units:** 14 Stable Core + 6 Premium Upside | expected TTM revenue $639,925, annualized volatility $28,674, risk-adjusted score 22.32.

## Risk-Adjusted Rationale

- Stable Core has the better average risk-adjusted return (0.89 vs 0.79).
- Premium Upside has the stronger revenue ceiling ($40,510 vs $28,348 average TTM revenue per listing), but its monthly revenue is materially more variable.
- A blended portfolio gives the host a reliable occupancy base while still participating in higher-rate premium demand.
